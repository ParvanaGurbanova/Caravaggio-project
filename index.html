Introduction

We started with this query to identify Cultural Properties in ArCo related to the iconography of The Calling of Saint Matthew. The goal was to verify whether the Knowledge Graph contains explicit references to the artwork associated with Caravaggio, using FILTER and REGEX for keyword search such as “vocazione di San Matteo”, “san matteo”, and “caravaggio”.

We used DISTINCT to remove duplicates caused by multilingual labels (Italian and English). The results showed heterogeneous cultural objects related to Saint Matthew, including paintings and sculptures.

Some results referred to Caravaggio, while others included similar names such as “da Caravaggio” or stylistic attributions. This introduced ambiguity in the dataset.

After identifying candidate Cultural Properties, we refined the analysis by focusing on author attribution to distinguish Michelangelo Merisi da Caravaggio from other similar entries.

Methodology

We explored the ArCo Knowledge Graph using SPARQL endpoint and ontology documentation.

The methodology followed three steps: data extraction, gap identification, and semantic enrichment.

SPARQL Queries & Results

Example query used for exploration:

SELECT DISTINCT ?p ?o
WHERE {
  ?x ?p ?o .
  FILTER regex(str(?o), "caravaggio", "i")
}

This query helped identify Cultural Properties related to Caravaggio.

Gaps

We identified ambiguity in the dataset due to similar names (Caravaggio vs da Caravaggio). Lexical matching alone was not sufficient.

LLM Usage

Large Language Models were used to interpret descriptions, extract subjects, and disambiguate entities.

RDF Enrichment

We enriched the knowledge graph by generating RDF triples. 

@prefix arco:  .
@prefix a-cd:  .

ex:Artwork a arco:CulturalProperty ;
    a-cd:hasSubject ex:SaintMatthew .

Challenges

Main challenges included ambiguity in entity names, multilingual labels, and noisy SPARQL results.

Conclusion

The combination of SPARQL queries, LLM assistance, and RDF enrichment improved the quality of extracted knowledge and reduced ambiguity in the dataset.
