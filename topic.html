Topic Overview

This project focuses on the analysis and semantic enrichment of the artwork The Calling of Saint Matthew by Michelangelo Merisi da Caravaggio (1599–1600). The painting is one of the most important works of Baroque art and is located in the Contarelli Chapel of San Luigi dei Francesi in Rome.

The artwork depicts the biblical moment when Jesus calls Matthew, a tax collector, to become one of his apostles. The dramatic use of light and shadow (chiaroscuro) is a defining feature of Caravaggio’s artistic style.

Artistic Importance

The Calling of Saint Matthew represents a turning point in Baroque art. Caravaggio rejected idealized religious imagery and instead used realistic human figures and everyday settings to convey spiritual meaning.

The strong contrast between light and darkness emphasizes the moment of divine intervention, making the scene emotionally powerful and visually dramatic.

Why This Artwork?

This artwork was selected because it is represented in the ArCo Knowledge Graph, but some semantic information is incomplete or ambiguous.

This makes it an ideal case study for exploring how Knowledge Graphs can be enriched using Large Language Models and SPARQL-based extraction methods.

Research Focus

  • Analyze representation of the artwork in ArCo Knowledge Graph
  • Identify missing or incomplete semantic information
  • Use SPARQL queries for data extraction
  • Generate RDF triples using Large Language Models
  • Compare and evaluate LLM outputs
