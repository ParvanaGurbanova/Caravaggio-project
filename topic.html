Topic Overview

This project focuses on the semantic analysis and enrichment of The Calling of Saint Matthew (1599–1600) by Michelangelo Merisi da Caravaggio. The painting is one of the most important masterpieces of the Baroque period and is located in the Contarelli Chapel in Rome.

The artwork represents the moment when Jesus Christ calls Matthew to become one of his apostles.

Artistic Significance

Caravaggio revolutionized religious painting by introducing realism, dramatic lighting, and everyday human expressions into sacred scenes.

The strong use of chiaroscuro (light vs darkness) emphasizes divine intervention and guides the viewer’s attention directly to the key moment in the narrative.

Reason for Selection

We selected this artwork because it is partially represented in the ArCo Knowledge Graph, but its semantic description is incomplete or ambiguous.

This makes it an ideal case study for exploring Knowledge Graph enrichment using Large Language Models and SPARQL-based extraction.

Research Focus

  • Analyze ArCo Knowledge Graph representation of the artwork
  • Identify missing or ambiguous semantic relations
  • Use SPARQL queries for structured extraction
  • Generate RDF triples using Large Language Models
  • Compare outputs from different LLMs
